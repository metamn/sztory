Gaming and Animation

In the nineties with the success of the Windows operating system computers turned into game stations. Remember Wolfenstein 3D and Doom?
As the browser emerged into a new universal platform the need to make it gaming friendly was an urge.

Game programmer Jonathan Gay saw this opportunity and created a web environment where desktop computer like animations and games were possible.

Flash and multimedia

His product and company was acquired by creative software maker Macromedia, later by Adobe and became de-facto standard for web based interactive media.

Flash was fresh air for web and made everybody happy. Advertisers could have now animated banners, web designers and developers could create fully animated websites, games found home in the browser, scientists show us magic with animated visualizations, print designers could embed their best of typography and fonts into websites.

Web content become rich. After ten years 96% of all browsers were using Flash and there were 2 million professionals using it.

The End Of Flash

Some success stories come to an end. More often those who ignore the basic rules. Flash violated the first rule of the internet, the openness. Like a black box inside the web browser nobody knew what's inside. Even Google could not figure out what a Flash site is and penalized them in search results. The mobile revolution took on surprise Flash and it's parent company Adobe with no community behind it's closed source code. In 2011 the black box grew over the mobile device and nobody could save this wonder technology from disappearance.
