The company

The Chicago—more precisely—worldwide based, quasi officeless company 37Signals is pretty unique on the web palette.

It's a modern hybrid influencing the web in many ways. Maybe their biggest impact was demistifying business and make web people entrepreneurs, but also their contribution to decompose technology in smaller stacks will be probably a historical move.

The idols

The company was founded by Jason Fried, a web designer who realized building web applications instead of web sites is a viable business. Moreover, his thinking distrupted the entire establishment introducing new concepts like self-founding startups, software as a service, simple and small means power.

Together with partner and rockstar developer David Heinemeier Hansson they've brought modernism to web, from all aspects.

Modernism is built on minimalism and asymmetry. In 37Signals' case that meant building a company and a product from bottom up, with no external venture capital, by their own and their customers' forces. Aside this minimal business model their take on the market was asymmetric: a small, agile team took relentlessly over a giant (Microsoft) napstering their project planning and management division.

In their entire activity and presence they've decomposed everything, trim the fat, and put together something revolutionary which imprinted people's mind. From now on people were not afraid to quit their daily job and start a company; instead of running after capital to start up they've created a simple, minimum viable product in a few weeks and started selling directly to customers; the revenue was invested back into the product instead of fancy offices, expensive CEOs and advertising. The product became the message.

This new paradigm also shifted web technology. From web servers to databases and programming languages everything was reformed. Soon all web technology became like the web itself: open, built on little components, organized into powerful and evolvable networks.

Thanks 37Signals, Welcome Modern Web.
