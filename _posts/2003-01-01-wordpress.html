While TYPO3 made content management easy for enterprises Wordpress made it easy for people starting a new era in web history: blogging, or self-publishing.

The easy to use and fully customizable nature of Wordpress made non-technical people enjoy content creation for the web. Later this elevated to an alarming level for the media, advertising and education industry they've been napstered by high quality and fast, better interconnected content created by large, well-educated masses.

Wordpress vs. old media google trends

Wordpress was differrent than anything else before at two fronts: it made content personal and viral. At a moment every company or every professional had a blog and a community built around through a smart commenting system. Every content piece interconnected via feeds let people be notified real-time about what's happening with their content and in that part of the blogosphere they were interested. Everybody could become a content hero overnight.

Self publishing cut the middlemen. You don't had to buy a book about a hot topic when you could read posts of the author on the blog. Then find out from comments who else is expert in the subject, or, check out the blogroll—a small network of most important related authors and blogs—for more.

Even Google went ignored when searching for knowledge; Technorati, Delicious, Stumble Upon & co were the new search engines of this new medium.

web 2.0

Wordress was the fuel of the web 2.0 revolution which lead to social networks and the real time web.
